Hello! I’m Martina Tan, a UX practitioner who designs at the intersection of product strategy, storytelling, and social impact. I’m currently finishing my capstone at CMU MHCI.

I'm from Mount Laurel, New Jersey, but I'm currently earning a Master in Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University.

In 2021, I graduated with two degrees in Cognitive and Brain Science and Interdisciplinary Studio Art, plus a Computer Science minor, as a Combined Degree student at Tufts and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts.

Design and art have been my most persistent passions since elementary school, when I would jump at any chance to meticulously sketch and hand-write all of my project posters. I still love all manners of creating by hand, but I've also expanded my toolbox into the digital realms of programming and prototyping. I hope to have a career in Human-Computer Interaction or UX Design while maintaining a steady art practice.

I find joy in collaborating with people of diverse backgrounds throughout my design process, with the goal of achieving solutions that are inclusive, audience-conscious and accessible.

When I'm not sketching, I'm usually thinking about tree bark, scrolling on Tumblr, or consuming speculative fiction/fantasy stories. I use she/her pronouns.
