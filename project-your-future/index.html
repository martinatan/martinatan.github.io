Project Your Future

Teaching Carnegie Museum visitors to reflect on their role in Earth’s climate future

Duration

Jan - Apr 2022 (4 mo)

Client

Carnegie Museum of Natural History

Team

2 designers - Nicole Araya and Jailyn Zabala

Skills

Expert Interviews, Generative Research, Data Analysis, Experience Mapping, Interactive Exhibition Design, Wizard of Oz, Think Aloud, Evaluation Metrics

Overview

For a design course on Learning in Museums at Carnegie Mellon University, my team collaborated with a local Pittsburgh museum to design a learning experience that would bring an Anthropocene perspective to their natural science galleries. Working from an open-ended design brief, we conducted generative field research and prototyped an exhibition concept that taught systems thinking and human stewardship through an interactive, animated projection.

I led the development of an experience map to describe the visitor touchpoints of our concept. I was also responsible for writing interview guides that appropriately measured visitor attitudes and knowledge about the human systems that drive climate change, and potential actions to mitigate climate risk.

Our team demonstrated this prototype to an audience of over a dozen exhibitions staff at the museum. Using analysis from our evaluative testing, we made design recommendations for staff to reference when tackling Anthropocene values in future exhibits.
